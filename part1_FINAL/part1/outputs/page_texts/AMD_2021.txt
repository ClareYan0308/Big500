- The goal is a 20% reduction in absolute scope 1+2 GHG emissions from 2014 – 2020. The baseline value is 52,183 MTCO2e, and the goal value is 41,746 MTCO2e. In 2019, performance against the goal was 23% below the baseline based on scope 1+2 emissions of 40,100 MTCO2e.
- AMD estimates or receives estimates from third parties for several scope 3 GHG emission sources. Scope 3 are indirect emissions (not included in scope 2) that occur in AMD’s supply chain, product use, and travel activities.
- Starting with 2014 reporting data, AMD estimates annual scope 3 GHG emissions from the energy use of certain products sold. Annual estimates approximate typical energy use of the AMD notebook and desktop Accelerated Processing Units (APUs), the average US emission factor of electricity, the estimated years of service life, and the approximate number of units sold per year.
- AMD receives estimated data on energy use, GHG emissions, water use, hazardous and non-hazardous waste from our wafer foundries and outsourced semiconductor assembly and test (OSAT) providers that is attributed to AMD products based on a manufacturing index.
- Product logistics / shipping emissions is provided by third party transportation vendors in accordance with the GHG Protocol.
- In 2016, there was a one-time disposal of non-operational waste of 966 tons of non-hazardous soil and water from an AMD Superfund site in California. With this one-time disposal, the 2016 waste generated would be 1,539 metric tons, and the percentage of waste diverted from landfill would have been 26%
- NHW diversion rate is the estimated percentage of NHW generated that is kept from the landfill due to recycling, composting or other means.
- All wastewater is discharged into municipal wastewater treatment plants according to water quality permitting.
